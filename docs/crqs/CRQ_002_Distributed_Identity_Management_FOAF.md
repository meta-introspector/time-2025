# CRQ-002: Distributed Identity Management and FOAF Integration

## Title
Distributed Identity Management and FOAF Integration

## Status
Open

## Date
October 3, 2025

## Description
The **singular** task of integrating **Friend of a Friend (FOAF)** identity management, grounding human and digital agents within the quantifiable structure of the lattice, as agents are the core actors moving through the lattice points (quasifibers).

### Context
In a distributed and agent-centric system, robust and verifiable identity management is crucial. This CRQ focuses on integrating the Friend of a Friend (FOAF) ontology to establish a decentralized identity framework, allowing both human and digital agents to define and manage their identities within the project's architectural lattice.

## Goal
1.  Design and implement a distributed identity management system based on the FOAF ontology.
2.  Enable human and digital agents to create, manage, and verify their identities within the system.
3.  Integrate the FOAF-based identity system with the project's core architectural components.

## Proposed Solution / Next Steps
1.  Research existing distributed identity management solutions and FOAF implementations.
2.  Develop a schema for representing agent identities using FOAF.
3.  Implement a decentralized identity registry or a similar mechanism.
4.  Integrate the identity management system with agent communication protocols and access control mechanisms.

## Impact
*   Enhanced security and trustworthiness through verifiable distributed identities.
*   Improved interoperability and collaboration between human and digital agents.
*   Provides a foundational layer for advanced agent-based services and interactions.
