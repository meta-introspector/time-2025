The Bott periodicity theorem, discovered by Raoul Bott, describes a periodicity in the homotopy groups of classical groups, which is fundamental to K-theory and stable homotopy groups of spheres. It manifests as a period-2 phenomenon for the unitary group and a period-8 phenomenon for the orthogonal and quaternionic symplectic groups. The theorem can be formulated in various ways, including through loop spaces and classifying spaces, and has connections to Clifford algebras.[1] Sources: [1] Bott periodicity theorem - Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bott_periodicity)
