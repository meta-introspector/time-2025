The number 17 is a natural number that follows 16 and precedes 18. It is a prime number and holds several notable properties in mathematics and other fields[1]. In mathematics, 17 is a Leyland number and a Fermat prime, which means a regular heptadecagon (17-sided polygon) can be constructed using only a compass and an unmarked ruler[1]. This discovery by Carl Friedrich Gauss influenced his decision to pursue mathematics[1]. Additionally, 17 is the minimum number of "givens" required for a Sudoku puzzle to have a unique solution[1]. Geometrically, there are 17 crystallographic space groups in two dimensions, also known as wallpaper groups, representing the possible symmetry types for wallpaper[1]. It is also the number of combinations of regular polygons that can completely fill a plane vertex[1]. In three-dimensional space, there are 17 distinct fully supported stellations generated by an icosahedron[1]. In other fields, 17 is the number of syllables in a traditional Japanese haiku (5, 7, 5 syllables per line)[1]. In psychology, 17 has been described as "the least random number" because, in studies asking people to choose a random number between 1 and 20, 17 is frequently chosen[1].
