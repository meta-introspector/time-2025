# Idea of 3: The Triplet (Ternary Relationship or Simple Composition)

## Concept
Represents a ternary relationship or a simple composition of three distinct elements. This could be an object with three fields, an array with three elements, or a more abstract grouping of three related `ExprObject`s. It signifies a basic level of structured composition.

## Representation
This concept would primarily manifest within `ExprObject::Object` or `ExprObject::Array` where the number of direct children (fields or elements) is constrained to three.

## Properties

1.  **Components**: Three distinct `ExprObject`s.
2.  **Relationship**: The nature of the connection between the three components (e.g., ordered triplet, three-part composition).
3.  **Location/Offset**: The byte range encompassing all three components within the original raw log string.
4.  **Type**: The composite type (e.g., a three-field object, a three-element array).
5.  **Tags/Constraints**: Could be tagged as `triplet`, `three_part_composition`.

## How this relates to `zos`

*   `zos[3]` (3): Directly corresponds to this concept, representing a structure with exactly three primary components.

This idea extends the combinatorial principle from pairs to triplets, allowing for slightly more complex, yet still fundamental, structured groupings within the log data.
