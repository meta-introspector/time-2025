# Idea of 13: The Tridecad (Extended Group Composition)

## Concept
Represents an extended group composition of thirteen distinct elements or a structure with thirteen primary components. This could be an object with thirteen fields, an array with thirteen elements, or a more abstract grouping of thirteen related `ExprObject`s. It signifies a further expansion of structured composition, allowing for more comprehensive information clusters.

## Representation
This concept would primarily manifest within `ExprObject::Object` or `ExprObject::Array` where the number of direct children (fields or elements) is constrained to thirteen.

## Properties

1.  **Components**: Thirteen distinct `ExprObject`s.
2.  **Relationship**: The nature of the connection between the thirteen components.
3.  **Location/Offset**: The byte range encompassing all thirteen components within the original raw log string.
4.  **Type**: The composite type (e.g., a thirteen-field object, a thirteen-element array).
5.  **Tags/Constraints**: Could be tagged as `tridecad`, `thirteen_part_composition`.

## How this relates to `zos`

*   `zos[7]` (13): Directly corresponds to this concept, representing a structure with exactly thirteen primary components.

This idea allows for the representation of even more detailed and complex information clusters within a single structural unit, while still adhering to the `zos` principle of constrained complexity and providing a clear boundary for analysis.
