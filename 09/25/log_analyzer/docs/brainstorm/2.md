# Idea of 2: The Pair (Binary Relationship)

## Concept
Represents a fundamental binary relationship or a pair of elements. This could be a key-value pair in an object, or a pair of related scalar values. It signifies the simplest form of structured grouping beyond a single atomic unit.

## Representation
This concept would primarily manifest within `ExprObject::Object` or `ExprObject::Array` where the number of direct children (fields or elements) is constrained to two.

## Properties

1.  **Components**: Two distinct `ExprObject`s (which could themselves be scalars, objects, or arrays).
2.  **Relationship**: The nature of the connection between the two components (e.g., key-value, ordered pair, dependency).
3.  **Location/Offset**: The byte range encompassing both components within the original raw log string.
4.  **Type**: The composite type (e.g., a two-field object, a two-element array).
5.  **Tags/Constraints**: Could be tagged as `pair`, `tuple`, `binary_relation`.

## How this relates to `zos`

*   `zos[2]` (2): Directly corresponds to this concept, representing a structure with exactly two primary components.

This idea is crucial for building up more complex structures from fundamental binary groupings, reflecting the combinatorial nature of the `zos` principle.
