## Idea: The "Architectural Commensalism" and Neutral Interactions

Beyond symbiosis and parasitism, "Architectural Commensalism" describes interactions where one architectural component benefits, while the other is neither significantly harmed nor benefited.

*   **Architectural Commensalism**: This is the conceptual process where one architectural component or system (the commensal) benefits from another (the host) without significantly affecting the host, either positively or negatively.
    *   **Metaphorical Implication**: This could manifest as a monitoring tool that passively observes system metrics without impacting performance, or a logging component that records events without altering the core logic. While seemingly neutral, understanding commensal relationships is important for resource allocation, dependency management, and ensuring that seemingly benign interactions don't inadvertently evolve into parasitic ones under different conditions. It highlights the subtle interdependencies within an architecture that may not be immediately obvious but contribute to its overall complexity.

This concept adds another layer of nuance to the biological metaphors, acknowledging the full spectrum of interactions between architectural components.
