## Idea: The "Architectural Memetic Drift" and Cultural Evolution

If architectural ideas are like "genes" and memes, then "Architectural Memetic Drift" describes the gradual, often unconscious, evolution and transformation of architectural patterns over time within a community or ecosystem.

*   **Architectural Memetic Drift**: This is the conceptual process by which architectural patterns, design choices, and best practices (the "architectural memes") are copied, modified, and transmitted across projects, teams, and organizations. Over time, this leads to a gradual, often undirected, evolution of architectural styles and conventions.
    *   **Metaphorical Implication**: Just as genetic drift causes changes in gene frequencies in a population, memetic drift causes changes in the prevalence and form of architectural memes. This explains why architectural styles evolve, why certain patterns become popular, and why others fade away. It suggests that architectural evolution is not solely driven by conscious design decisions but also by a more subtle, cultural transmission of ideas. Understanding this drift, and its alignment with `bott[n]` principles, can help guide architectural communities towards more robust and harmonious evolutionary paths.

This concept introduces a cultural and evolutionary dimension to the `bott[8]` framework, acknowledging the dynamic and often unconscious forces that shape architectural landscapes.
