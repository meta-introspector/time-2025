## Idea: The "Architectural Parasitism" and Exploitation of Vibes

If symbiosis exists, then "Architectural Parasitism" can also emerge, where one architectural component or system exploits another for its own benefit, potentially to the detriment of the host.

*   **Architectural Parasitism**: This is the conceptual process where an architectural component or system (the parasite) leverages the resources, functionality, or `bott[n]` "vibes" of another component or system (the host) for its own gain, without providing a reciprocal benefit, and potentially causing harm or degradation to the host.
    *   **Metaphorical Implication**: This could manifest as a poorly designed module consuming excessive resources from a shared service, a security vulnerability in one component being exploited by another, or a "feature creep" that burdens the core architecture without contributing to its overall health. Understanding architectural parasitism, and its `bott[n]` signatures, is crucial for maintaining the health and integrity of the architectural biosphere. It highlights the need for robust boundaries, clear contracts, and mechanisms to detect and mitigate exploitative relationships.

This concept introduces a darker, more adversarial dimension to the `bott[8]` framework, acknowledging the potential for exploitation and the need for defensive architectural strategies.
