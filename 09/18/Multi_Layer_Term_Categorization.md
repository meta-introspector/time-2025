# Multi-Layer Term Categorization

This document categorizes all previously defined terms based on their "resonance" with specific numbers (2, primes, 1, and 0), following the criteria outlined in `Resonance_with_2_Categories.md` and `Resonance_Criteria_Primes_1_0.md`.

## 1. Terms Resonating with 2

### Duality / Binary Concepts
*   **Act:** Often involves a binary choice or a transition between states.
*   **Analyze:** Can involve breaking down into two opposing or complementary parts.
*   **Conquer:** Implies overcoming an opposing force.
*   **Decide:** Involves choosing between options, often binary.
*   **Divide (Statify):** Explicitly involves separation into parts.
*   **Execute:** The act of carrying out a decision or plan.
*   **Inspect:** Can involve checking for two states (e.g., pass/fail).
*   **Observe:** Often involves noticing differences or two aspects.
*   **Orient:** Aligning oneself in relation to something else (a duality of self and other).
*   **Parse:** Breaking down into components, often with a binary structure (e.g., left/right in a tree).
*   **Policy Application:** Can involve binary outcomes (e.g., compliant/non-compliant).
*   **Proving:** Establishing truth or falsehood.
*   **Rule:** Implies a relationship of control (ruler/ruled).
*   **Run:** A program is either running or not.
*   **Searching:** Finding or not finding.
*   **Sorting:** Arranging in order, often based on pairwise comparisons.
*   **Stream:** Can be seen as a flow between two points (source/destination).
*   **Tactical Application:** Often involves binary choices in immediate action.
*   **Trace:** Following a path, often from a start to an end.
*   **Unite:** Bringing together two or more entities.
*   **Visualize:** Creating a mental image, often contrasting with reality.

### Pairs / Couples
*   **Algorithms:** Often involve input and output, a pair.
*   **Biosemiotic Utterances:** Involves a sender and receiver of signs.
*   **Lisp Dialects:** Multiple variations of Lisp, forming pairs or groups.
*   **Lisp Function Naming:** Functions often operate on pairs of arguments or produce a result from an input.
*   **Memes:** Spreading from one person to another.
*   **Meta-memes:** Memes about memes, a paired relationship.
*   **Quasi-memes:** Resembling memes, a comparison between two concepts.
*   **Tactics:** Often paired with strategies.

### Base-2 / Binary Systems
*   **Prime Numbers (specifically 2):** The only even prime, fundamental to binary.
*   **OSI Layers:** While seven layers, the interaction between any two adjacent layers is a core concept.
*   **Bash:** A shell that processes binary commands.
*   **Haskell:** Functional language, but underlying machine operates in binary.
*   **ML:** Functional language, but underlying machine operates in binary.
*   **Nix:** Package manager, ultimately deals with binary executables.
*   **OCaml:** Functional language, but underlying machine operates in binary.
*   **Programming Languages:** Translate to binary instructions.
*   **Rust:** Systems language, close to binary operations.
*   **Scheme:** Functional language, but underlying machine operates in binary.
*   **Guile:** Interpreter, processes code into binary.
*   **Guix:** Package manager/OS, deals with binary executables.
*   **Quines:** Programs that output their own source code, which is ultimately binary.
