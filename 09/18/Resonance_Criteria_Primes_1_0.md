# Resonance Criteria for Primes, 1, and 0

This document outlines the creative criteria used to categorize terms based on their "resonance" with specific prime numbers, and then with 1 and 0.

## Resonance with Prime Numbers

### Resonance with 3
Terms related to trinity, three-part structures, synthesis (e.g., thesis-antithesis-synthesis), three-dimensional space, or concepts often grouped in threes.

### Resonance with 5
Terms related to the five senses, five-part structures, pentagons, the human hand/body (five fingers/toes), or concepts often grouped in fives.

### Resonance with 7
Terms related to seven-part structures, cycles (e.g., 7 days a week), luck, completeness (e.g., 7 colors of rainbow, 7 notes in a musical scale), or concepts often grouped in sevens.

### Resonance with 11
Terms related to complexity, prime nature, concepts that are often seen as "beyond" simple structures, or those that introduce an element of unexpectedness or higher order.

### Resonance with 13
Terms related to superstition, complexity, concepts that are often seen as "unlucky" or challenging, or those that break established patterns.

### Resonance with 17
Terms related to unique prime properties, concepts that are often seen as distinct, individual, or representing a specific, irreducible quality.

### Resonance with 19
Terms related to unique prime properties, concepts that are often seen as distinct, individual, or representing a specific, irreducible quality, similar to 17 but with its own unique prime essence.

### Resonance with 23
Terms related to unique prime properties, concepts that are often seen as distinct, individual, or representing a specific, irreducible quality, similar to 17 and 19 but with its own unique prime essence.

## Resonance with 1
Terms related to singularity, unity, origin, the fundamental unit, uniqueness, indivisibility, or the starting point of a sequence.

## Resonance with 0
Terms related to absence, void, null, nothingness, the beginning/end of a cycle, potential (as in a blank slate), or initialization.
