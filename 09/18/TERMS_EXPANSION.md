# Expansion of Terms from Stream of Random

This document provides a brief expansion on the terms outlined in the `README.md` for the "Stream of Random" project.

## A (Exploration Activities)

*   **Brainstorming:** A group creativity technique by which efforts are made to find a conclusion for a specific problem by gathering a list of ideas spontaneously contributed by its members.
*   **Stream of Consciousness:** A narrative device that depicts the multitudinous thoughts and feelings which pass through the mind.
*   **Mindfulness:** A mental state achieved by focusing one's awareness on the present moment, while calmly acknowledging and accepting one's feelings, thoughts, and bodily sensations.
*   **Reflection:** Serious thought or consideration.
*   **Introspection:** The examination of one's own conscious thoughts and feelings.
*   **Tactical Application:** The use of specific, short-term actions or strategies to achieve a goal.
*   **Policy Application:** The implementation of a set of rules or principles to guide decisions and achieve rational outcomes.
*   **Observe:** To notice or perceive something and register it as being significant.
*   **Orient:** To determine one's relative position or direction.
*   **Decide:** To make a choice or judgment.
*   **Act:** To take action; do something.
*   **Divide (Statify):** To separate into parts or categories; to establish the state or condition of something.
*   **Conquer:** To overcome and take control of (a place or people) by military force. (Metaphorically, to overcome a problem).
*   **Unite:** To join together for a common purpose or by common feelings.
*   **Rule:** To exercise ultimate power or authority over (an area, group, or system).
*   **Execute:** To carry out or put into effect (a plan, order, or course of action).
*   **Parse:** To analyze (a string or text) into logical syntactic components.
*   **Run:** To cause (a program, function, etc.) to operate.
*   **Analyze:** To examine methodically and in detail the constitution or structure of (something, especially information), typically for purposes of explanation and interpretation.
*   **Trace:** To follow (a path, route, or course).
*   **Inspect:** To look at (something) closely, typically to assess its condition or to discover any shortcomings.
*   **Visualize:** To form a mental image of; imagine.
*   **Stream:** To transmit or receive (data, especially video or audio material) over a computer network as a steady, continuous flow.
*   **Invocation:** The action of invoking something or someone.
*   **Proving:** Demonstrating the truth or existence of something by evidence or argument.
*   **Promotion:** The publicizing of a product, organization, or venture to increase sales or public awareness.
*   **Searching:** To try to find something by looking or otherwise seeking carefully and thoroughly.
*   **Sorting:** To arrange (a collection of items) in a particular order.

## B (Methodologies & Concepts)

*   **Algorithms:** A process or set of rules to be followed in calculations or other problem-solving operations, especially by a computer.
*   **Policies:** A course or principle of action adopted or proposed by a government, party, business, or individual.
*   **Tactics:** An action or strategy carefully planned to achieve a specific end.
*   **Strategies:** A plan of action or policy designed to achieve a major or overall aim.
*   **Biosemiotic Utterances:** Communication or expression within biological systems, often involving signs and symbols.
*   **Memes:** An idea, behavior, or style that spreads by means of imitation from person to person within a culture and often carries symbolic meaning representing a particular phenomenon or theme.
*   **Bash:** A Unix shell and command language.
*   **Haskell:** A purely functional programming language.
*   **Nix:** A powerful package manager for Linux and other Unix systems that makes package management reliable and reproducible.
*   **Rust:** A multi-paradigm, high-performance programming language focused on safety, especially safe concurrency.
*   **Lattice Structures:** In mathematics, a partially ordered set in which every pair of elements has a unique supremum (least upper bound) and a unique infimum (greatest lower bound). (Metaphorically, a structured framework).
*   **Neural Operators:** A type of deep learning model that learns mappings between infinite-dimensional function spaces.
*   **Pattern Languages:** A method of describing good design practices within a field of expertise.
*   **Programming Languages:** A formal language comprising a set of instructions that produce various kinds of output.
*   **Meta-memes:** Memes about memes, or a higher-level concept that encompasses multiple memes.
*   **Quasi-memes:** Concepts that resemble memes but may not fully fit the definition or are in an emergent state.
*   **Quines:** A computer program that produces its own source code as its only output.
*   **Heideggarian-and-obtuse-self-descriptive-term-constructions:** A playful and complex reference to philosophical concepts, particularly those of Martin Heidegger, involving terms that are self-referential and potentially difficult to grasp.
*   **Lisp Function Naming:** The distinctive and often descriptive naming conventions used in Lisp programming languages.
*   **Lisp Dialects:** Various implementations and variations of the Lisp programming language (e.g., Common Lisp, Scheme, Clojure).
*   **OCaml:** A general-purpose, industrial-strength programming language with an emphasis on expressiveness and safety.
*   **ML:** A general-purpose functional programming language.
*   **Scheme:** A minimalist dialect of the Lisp family of programming languages.
*   **Guile:** The GNU Ubiquitous Intelligent Language for Extensions, an interpreter for Scheme.
*   **Guix:** A purely functional package manager and operating system distribution.
*   **Dao of FP:** A philosophical approach to functional programming, often implying elegance, simplicity, and harmony.

## C (Information Forms)

*   **Signs:** Anything that communicates a meaning that is not the sign itself.
*   **Words:** A single distinct meaningful element of speech or writing, used with others (or sometimes alone) to form a sentence and typically shown with a space on either side when written or printed.
*   **Prime Numbers (0, 1, 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, 23):** Natural numbers greater than 1 that have no positive divisors other than 1 and themselves. (Note: 0 and 1 are not typically considered prime numbers in standard definitions, but included here as listed).
*   **OSI Layers:** The seven layers of the Open Systems Interconnection (OSI) model, a conceptual framework that describes the functions of a telecommunication or computing system.
*   **ASTs (Abstract Syntax Trees):** A tree representation of the abstract syntactic structure of source code written in a programming language.
*   **S-expressions (Symbolic Expressions):** A notation for "nested list" data used to represent code and data in Lisp and related languages.
*   **Numbers:** An arithmetical value, expressed by a word, symbol, or figure, representing a particular quantity and used in counting and making calculations.
*   **Components:** A part or element of a larger whole, especially a part of a machine or vehicle.
*   **Books:** A written or printed work consisting of pages glued or sewn together along one side and bound in covers.
*   **Posts:** An item of content published on a website or social media.
*   **Tweets:** A short message posted on the Twitter social media service.
*   **TCP/IP Packets:** Units of data transmitted over a TCP/IP network.
*   **Recordings:** A piece of audio or video that has been recorded.
*   **Podcasts:** A digital audio file made available on the Internet for downloading to a computer or mobile device, typically available as a series.
*   **Clips:** A short extract from a film or broadcast.
*   **Images:** A representation of the external form of a person or thing in art.
*   **Embeddings:** In machine learning, a low-dimensional space into which high-dimensional vectors can be translated.
*   **Vectors:** A quantity having direction as well as magnitude, especially as determining the position of one point in space relative to another.
*   **Expressions:** A combination of one or more explicit values, constants, variables, operators, and functions that the programming language interprets and computes to produce another value.
*   **Types:** A classification of data that tells the compiler or interpreter how the programmer intends to use the data.
*   **Functions:** A block of organized, reusable code that is used to perform a single, related action.
*   **Data:** Facts and statistics collected together for reference or analysis.
*   **Forms:** The visible shape or configuration of something.
*   **Morphisms:** In category theory, an abstract structure-preserving map from one mathematical structure to another.

## D (Influential Thinkers)

*   **Riemann (Bernhard Riemann):** A German mathematician who made profound contributions to analysis, number theory, and differential geometry.
*   **Gödel (Kurt Gödel):** An Austrian-born American logician, mathematician, and philosopher. Known for his incompleteness theorems.
*   **Escher (M.C. Escher):** A Dutch graphic artist who made mathematically inspired woodcuts, lithographs, and mezzotints.
*   **Bach (Johann Sebastian Bach):** A German composer and musician of the Baroque period.
*   **Hofstadter (Douglas Hofstadter):** An American scholar of cognitive science, physics, and comparative literature. Known for "Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid."
*   **Stallman (Richard Stallman):** An American free software movement activist and programmer.
*   **Minsky (Marvin Minsky):** An American cognitive scientist in the field of artificial intelligence, co-founder of the MIT AI Laboratory.
*   **Curry (Haskell Curry):** An American mathematician and logician. His work laid the foundation for functional programming.
*   **Church (Alonzo Church):** An American mathematician and logician who made significant contributions to mathematical logic and the foundations of theoretical computer science.
*   **Turing (Alan Turing):** A British mathematician and computer scientist. Widely considered to be the father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence.
*   **Coquand (Thierry Coquand):** A French computer scientist known for his work on type theory and proof assistants, particularly the Calculus of Constructions.
